GLAZEVAULT — CORE PRODUCT POSITIONING & LONG-TERM PLATFORM DIRECTION

Do NOT evolve GlazeVault into a generic social media app for ceramics.

The long-term strength of GlazeVault is:

archive-first creative infrastructure.

CORE PHILOSOPHY

Most social platforms are:
share-first.

GlazeVault should remain:
record-first.

Artists should feel:

“I am building a lifelong ceramic archive.”
not
“I am posting content.”

CORE PRODUCT IDENTITY

GlazeVault =
private studio archive
+
public portfolio system
+
quiet exhibition network

The archive is the foundation.
The social layer emerges naturally from shared archives.

DO NOT OPTIMIZE FOR:
- virality
- engagement addiction
- infinite scrolling
- content farming
- creator pressure
- trending competition

DO NOT BUILD:
- loud notifications
- follower count obsession
- aggressive algorithmic feeds
- TikTok/Reels behavior
- engagement bait systems

DISCOVERY PHILOSOPHY

Discovery should feel like:
- wandering through open studios
- visiting exhibitions
- browsing artist monographs
- discovering collections slowly

NOT:
- doomscrolling
- rapid content consumption
- dopamine loops

DISCOVERY STRUCTURE

Prioritize:
- collection-first discovery
- featured collections
- recently updated archives
- aesthetic diversity
- slow exploration
- calm editorial pacing

Collections are more important than isolated posts.

Users should discover:
artist worlds,
not random disconnected content.

FOLLOW SYSTEM

Keep:
“Follow Artist”

But frame it as:
following an artist archive,
not following a social creator.

FOLLOW SHOULD ENABLE:
- new public collections
- new featured works
- exhibition updates

without social pressure.

SHARING PHILOSOPHY

Sharing should feel like:
sending someone to a digital exhibition.

NOT:
posting social content.

Core sharing units:
- Piece
- Collection
- Portfolio

Collections are especially important because they act like mini exhibitions.

LONG-TERM BUSINESS DIRECTION

The strongest business opportunity is NOT:
“Instagram for ceramics.”

The strongest opportunity is:
a trusted lifelong archive and portfolio infrastructure for ceramic artists.

Key value:
- artists build years of work inside the system
- glaze history accumulates
- firing knowledge accumulates
- collections evolve over time
- public portfolios emerge naturally

This creates:
high trust,
long retention,
and deep emotional attachment.

DESIGN PRINCIPLES

The app should feel:
- calm
- contemplative
- tactile
- editorial
- trustworthy
- artist-centered

Avoid:
- overstimulation
- clutter
- excessive gamification
- addictive mechanics

TARGET FEELING

GlazeVault should feel like:
a quiet digital ceramic library,
a personal studio archive,
and a contemporary exhibition space,
all connected through intentional sharing.
